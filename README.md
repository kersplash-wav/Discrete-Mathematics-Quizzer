# Discrete Mathematics Quizzer

## Inspiration
When we first encountered discrete mathematics in our undergraduate university courses, we felt extremely challenged by its contents. It felt as if many topics were too difficult to understand and that there was not enough practice to support our learnings. This wasn't a sentiment only just the two of us developers shared, but rather it was very common between other peers of ours - both at our university and outside of it.

## What our project does
Our project is a Discrete Mathematics Quizzer. With thousands of questions in categories ranging from combinatorics to trees to set theory, we cover all the areas of discrete mathematics an undergraduate university student should be expected to know and learn. Our project quizzes users on these areas: they're provided a question to which they must answer.

## What's next for the Discrete Mathematics Quizzer

This project aims to provide a vast amount of discrete mathematics questions tailored to learners, aspiring computer scientists, or anyone curious.
Each question is randomly selected and may contain random variables to ensure each question is unique, allowing for extra practice.

Each question has been assigned a difficulty rating (easy, medium, hard) to allow users to choose questions at a difficulty level they are most comfortable with.
These questions are also categorized into respective areas of discrete mathematics for further specialization in specific topics (ex. sets, proofs, recurrences).

This project was coded using Python and Streamlit.
